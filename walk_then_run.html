Season Wins vs. Walks, 1995-2004

Walk, then run

In baseball, a walk is earned when a batter does not swing at four pitches outside of the strike zone, giving them a free pass to first base. In many ways, this is just as valuable as getting a hit and making it to first base.

A reasonable assumption would be that teams who walk more often, win more often. This is, in fact, the case. It would logically follow that teams would spend money in ways that would get them more walks, right? Not so fast...
